As briefly mentioned on the index page; Arthur's Seat is frequently theorised as one of the possible locations for the legendary castle, Camelot and court of the Romano-British warrior-chief, King Arthur where he plotted with Knights of the Round Table.

Furthermore, the origin of how it came to be known as Arthur’s Seat is still unknown. However, William Maitland presented that the nae could have been a cypher of Àrd-na-Said, implying the "Height of Arrows" and then over the years transitioned to Arthur's Seat.
