Duddingston

The field is on Arthurs seat, just above duddingston Village. The field keeps marinated by the local villagers and volunteers and they have done amazing progress in the creation of a wide range of habitat to include semi-natural woodland, wetland, her-rich meadow, willow bed, tree nursery as well as an orchard.

Duddingston field produces a rich bio-diver link and public pathway between Duddingston Village Community, Meadow field Woods and Holyrood Park. This field is extremely popular and is used more and more by the local people, Edinburgh residents and tourists for access, activities (running walking, hiking and picnics).

Holyrood Palace

The palace of Holyrood house, better known as Holyrood Palace, is the official residence of the British monarch in Scotland, Queen Elizabeth the second. Holyrood Palace is located at the bottom of Arthur’s seat and has served as the principle royal residence in Scotland for over 400 years (since 16th century).

Holyrood Palace is associated with the Holyrood Park which has been referred to as the Queen’s Park or King’s Park depending is a royal park in central Edinburgh. Holyrood Park was formerly a 12th-century royal hunting estate but has now since became open to the public and is now used by locals, villagers, hikers and families looking for a fun day! The park also resides 3 lochs: St Margaret's Loch, Dunsapie Loch, and Duddingston Loch and The ruined St Anthony's Chapel stands above St Margaret's Loch (see photo above).
